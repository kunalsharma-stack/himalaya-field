D - Community & Traditional Knowledge
D1 Landscape Memory - How Has This Place Changed?
Solo prompt: What evidence in the landscape itself tells you how this place has changed - watermarks, dead trees, abandoned structures, eroded banks, changed vegetation lines?
Ask: "If you compare this place today with how you remember it as a child - or as your parents or grandparents described it - what feels most different?" Allow silence. Record verbatim.
🌿 Local / TEK layer (optional)
Ask the respondent: local names for this change, oral histories, place-names encoding past conditions, proverbs or sayings.
D2 First Moment of Change - When Did It Begin?
Based on physical evidence, vegetation signals, and any available records - what is your best estimate of when directional change began here?
Ask: "Is there a particular year, season, or event when you first noticed that things had started to change? What were the signs?" Probe gently: "Did others notice at the same time, or did you notice first?"
D3 Traditional Signs in Nature
What ecological indicators are visible here that a community member might traditionally use to read the season, weather, or ecosystem health?
Ask: "Are there sounds, colours, or patterns in nature that tell you what the season or weather will be? Has your ability to read these signs changed?" Probe: "What signals are no longer reliable? What new signs have appeared?"
🌿 Local names / TEK detail (optional)
Ask the respondent: local names for species / phenomena, seasonal calendars, proverbs or phrases used.
D4 The One Thing the World Must Not Forget
As sole witness at this site - what is the single observation, image, or change that this field record must preserve? Write slowly.
Ask (closing question - allow extended silence): "If you could document one thing about this place that the world must not forget - one story, one image, one change - what would it be?" Record word for word.
